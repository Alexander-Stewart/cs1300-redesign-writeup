Redesign: Grove Street Apparel

Project Background

This project had the goal of redesigning a website that had poor design practices. This redesign encompassed the entire design cycle, including hifi and lofi prototyping and a working html page. The website I chose to redesign is for Grove Street Apparel, an up and coming streetwear brand based of of Denton, Texas.

The Site

Their website, grovestreetapparel.net, includes a pretty familiar shopping site setup. It includes a home page, and several pages showcasing their clothing and a checkout system. For the sake of this project, we will be focusing on the homescreen of the site.

Finding Problems

After inspecting the home page, I noted what I thought about the sites usability, learnability, and memorability. They include:

  • Header picture will scroll over the page, covering half the screen consistently.
  • White background of header picture, hard to look at as you scroll.
  • Clothing items donâ€™t grow/shrink columns as screen size changes.
  • No styling per device type, not responsive and must scroll horizontally on iphone devices.
  • The memorable portion of the page, which is video, is found at the bottom. We see it last.
  • I learned that there is a new collection coming soon, and I assume that the clothes at the bottom are going to be the new styles.
  • Site is straightforward and simple, easy to use and move through content.

Accessibility

The webpage has generally good user accessibility. All of the main images have alternate text. Where the page falls behind in accessibility comes mainly from contrast and text sizing. The color scheme of the page has low contrast, making text and images hard to read on black backgrounds (this is very apparent in the header). Also, text across the site is not consistent for easily understanding breaks in sections.
